Coach Self-Awareness Quiz

Discover how well you understand your emotions and their impact on your athletes

1. During a crucial match, your team is losing and you notice your frustration building. What's your typical response?
Your team is down by two goals with 20 minutes left. You can feel your heart racing and tension in your shoulders. The players look deflated.
I take a deep breath, recognize my frustration, and focus on giving constructive tactical guidance to help the team refocus
I notice my frustration but sometimes struggle to keep it from showing in my voice when giving instructions
I feel the frustration but try to push through it, though I'm not always sure how it affects my communication
I get caught up in the moment and my frustration often comes through in my body language and tone
I react immediately to my frustration, often raising my voice or showing visible disappointment
2. After a player makes a costly mistake, you feel disappointed. How do you differentiate between your emotions and theirs?
Your star player misses a penalty that could have tied the game. You see their devastation, and you feel your own disappointment rising.
I clearly recognize my disappointment as separate from theirs and focus on supporting them through their emotions
I usually can tell the difference, though sometimes my disappointment influences how I respond to them
I'm aware there might be different emotions at play but don't always sort them out in the moment
I often feel like their disappointment intensifies mine, making it hard to separate what I'm feeling
I react to the situation as one emotional experience without distinguishing between our different feelings
3. When you're having a bad day personally, how aware are you of its impact on your coaching?
You've had a difficult morning - family stress, traffic, and you're running late to practice. The team is waiting for your usual energy and guidance.
I'm very aware of my personal state and consciously adjust my approach to ensure it doesn't negatively affect the team
I usually notice when I'm off and try to compensate, though it sometimes still shows through
I'm somewhat aware but don't always know how to prevent it from affecting my coaching
I often realize afterward that my mood affected the training session or team atmosphere
I rarely consider how my personal emotions might be impacting the team
4. How do you recognize when your communication style is affecting an athlete's confidence?
You've been giving frequent corrections to a player who's struggling with technique. You notice they seem more hesitant and less willing to take risks during drills.
I actively monitor how my words and tone are received and adjust immediately when I see signs of diminished confidence
I usually pick up on these cues and try to balance criticism with encouragement
I sometimes notice these signs but am not always sure how to adjust my approach effectively
I occasionally realize my communication may have been too harsh, usually after the fact
I focus mainly on getting my point across and don't often consider the impact on their confidence
5. When you feel anxiety or pressure before a big game, how do you manage it to avoid transferring it to your athletes?
It's the championship game. You feel the weight of expectations from parents, the school, and the players themselves. Your pre-game nerves are building.
I have clear strategies to manage my anxiety and consciously project calm confidence to help my athletes feel secure
I work on managing my nerves and usually succeed in staying calm around the team
I try to hide my anxiety but I'm not always successful, and I worry it might affect the team
My pre-game nerves often show through my behavior, and I think the team sometimes picks up on it
I feel the pressure intensely and don't really consider how my anxiety might be affecting the athletes
